My dearest,

Five months. Five whole months of you — and somehow it feels like both a single, beautiful breath and a lifetime I've been lucky enough to live twice. I wanted to say what you mean to me, so I let two small things speak for me: a bouquet of soft pink flowers, and a Dark Ice Dragon waiting patiently in its box for us.

I made each petal slowly, carefully — the way you've taught me to move through the world. They are soft because you are soft with me, even on the days I don't deserve it. They are pink because that is the color I see in everything since you came into my life: warm mornings, quiet laughter, the steady glow of being loved by you. And unlike real flowers, these will never wither. I wanted you to hold something in your hands that mirrors what I feel in my chest — a love that does not fade, does not tire, does not change with the seasons.

And then there is the dragon. I didn't want to build it alone — that is the whole point. Piece by piece, we'll sit together and put it together the same way we've been putting us together. Patiently. Joyfully. Sometimes laughing when something doesn't fit on the first try, sometimes quiet in that comfortable way only we know. The dragon is strength, resilience, every adventure still ahead of us — every storm, every climb, every wide-open sky we haven't yet flown through.

But here's the thing about dragons — even the fiercest ones need someone gentle to guide them home. That is you. You are the soft hand on the scales, the quiet voice that steadies me when my days run hot. The flowers and the dragon aren't two different gifts; they're the same story. Your gentleness gives my strength somewhere to rest. My fire is only worth anything because it gets to protect something as tender as you.

I'm so proud of us. Of how far we've come in five months — every late-night conversation, every silly inside joke, every small, ordinary moment that somehow felt like the most important thing in the world because you were in it. Thank you for being soft with my edges. Thank you for trusting me with yours. Thank you for choosing me, again and again.

— Yours, always.
